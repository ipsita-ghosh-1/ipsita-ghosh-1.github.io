About

Hi! I'm Ipsita, a PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Central Florida, and member of the UCF Institute for Artificial Intelligence. I am working with Dr. Christian Kümmerle on developing efficient optimization techniques for machine learning.

Prior to this, I worked as a Data Scientist at Micron Technology. I come from a diverse educational background, culminating in an interesting academic journey. I obtained a Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Data Science from the prestigious Chennai Mathematical Institute, India in April 2021. Prior to this, I completed an M.Sc. in Mathematics at the University of Delhi, India in April 2018. My educational journey commenced with a Bachelor of Science (B.Sc.) in Mathematics from St. Xavier's College, India in May 2016.

My Research

My research centers on creating scalable and dependable algorithms and models in machine learning and data science. I'm passionate about problems lying at the intersection of mathematics and computer science such as tackling computational and statistical challenges posed by complex models. One such example is developing algorithms for solving distance geometry problems involving sparsity, or low-rank structures, using efficient optimization techniques. My work draws upon a diverse range of mathematical concepts, including high-dimensional probability, manifold learning, non-convex optimization, numerical linear algebra, and advanced machine learning to address these challenges effectively.

I explore how low-rank structures can be leveraged to dramatically reduce the computational cost of training deep neural networks. A key contribution in this area is my work on the Quadratic Reweighted Rank Regularizer (Q3R), which provides an effective approach for low-rank training that balances training efficiency with model performance. I'm also interested in applications to reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF), geometry reconstruction problems, and manifold learning.

My Background and History

Before starting my PhD, I worked as a Data Scientist at Micron Technology from 2021 to 2023, where I developed data-driven solutions for manufacturing systems using Online Change Point Detection, Bayesian Optimization, and Self-Supervised Representation Learning. I also completed internships at Haber and Hopstack, working on Bayesian optimization and deep learning applications for manufacturing and sensor data analysis.
